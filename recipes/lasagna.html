Lasagna

Description

This Vegetarian Tomato and Greens Lasagna is an elevated, comforting twist on the classic Heirloom Tomato and Greens Lasagna. It pairs layers of tender pasta and rich ricotta with fresh, wilted greens and ripe, juicy tomatoes.

Ingredients:

    For the greens and ricotta filling:

  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 10 oz baby spinach, kale, or chard (washed and roughly chopped)
  • 16 oz whole-milk ricotta cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 4 oz goat cheese or mozzarella
  • 1 large egg, lightly beaten
  • Salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg

    For the assembly:

  • 3-4 cups marinara or tomato sauce
  • 2-3 medium ripe tomatoes (like heirloom or Roma, thinly sliced)
  • 8-10 oz lasagna noodles (fresh sheets or no-boil)
  • 8 oz fresh mozzarella, thinly sliced

Steps:

  1. Wilt the Greens: Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the greens and sauté until fully wilted (about 5 minutes). Transfer to a paper towel and press out excess moisture so your lasagna doesn't get watery.
  2. Mix the Cheese: In a medium bowl, stir together the ricotta, 1/2 cup of the Parmesan cheese, the egg, and a pinch of salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  3. Layer the Lasagna: Preheat your oven to 375F(190c). Coat the bottom of a 9 X 13-inch casserole dish with a thin layer of marinara sauce. Add a layer of noodles, followed by a third of the ricotta mixture, some sliced mozzarella, half of your greens, and half of the tomato slices. Repeat this layering process twice, finishing with a final layer of noodles topped with remaining sauce, sliced tomatoes, and mozzarella.
  4. Bake: Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake for 35-40 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for another 15 minutes, until the top is bubbling and golden. Let it stand for 10 minutes before slicing
